## Case Study Definition
This case study focuses on helping individuals, families, and shared households identify the borough on the Island of Montreal that best matches their rental needs. By combining housing prices, safety, accessibility, commute time, and access to public services, the project delivers a data-driven decision-support dashboard.

## Problem Statement
Finding a suitable place to rent in Montreal is complex due to wide variation in prices, safety, commute times, and access to schools and daycare. Information is fragmented across multiple sources, making objective comparison difficult.

## Business Objective
The objective is to design and deliver a dashboard that consolidates multiple data sources to help renters identify the most suitable borough based on individual priorities.

Key goals include:
- Simplifying rental location decision-making
- Enabling standardized comparisons
- Supporting personalized prioritization
- Identifying balanced boroughs
- Supporting landlords in competitive pricing decisions

## Target Users
- First-time renters
- Families with children
- Shared households
- Newcomers and relocators
- Older adults
- Landlords
- Real estate agencies

## Key Use Cases
- Compare rental prices by borough
- Assess walkability and transit access
- Evaluate safety using infraction rates
- Analyze access to schools, hospitals, and daycare
- Rank boroughs using user-defined weights
- Identify affordable or high-return locations

## Dashboard Success Metrics (KPIs)
### User Experience
- Fewer steps to identify a preferred borough
- Clear explanation of rankings
- Easy filtering and comparison

### Data Quality
- Full borough coverage
- Transparent data sources
- Timely refresh cycles

### Analytical Value
- Meaningful differentiation between boroughs
- Logical weighted rankings
- Alignment with user priorities

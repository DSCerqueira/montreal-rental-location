Montreal Rental Analytics · Business Case

Business Case

Executive Summary

This project delivers a data-driven dashboard to help renters, families, newcomers, and shared households identify the borough on the Island of Montreal that best fits their needs. By consolidating rental prices, safety, commute times, and access to schools, daycare, and public services, the dashboard enables objective comparison and prioritization of boroughs based on individual preferences. Success is measured by ease of use, data reliability, and meaningful insights that guide informed rental decisions. It showcases the end-to-end process—from data gathering and transformation to modeling and visualization.

Introduction

Finding a suitable rental on the Island of Montreal is a complex task for individuals, families, and shared households. Factors such as rental prices, safety, commute times, and access to essential services like schools and daycare vary widely across boroughs. This information is often scattered across multiple sources, making it challenging for renters to objectively compare locations and identify the areas that best meet their needs and priorities. Although this information is available online, there is currently no tool that integrates all these factors and evaluates locations according to individual requirements.

Business Objectives

The objective of this case study is to design and deliver a data-driven dashboard that consolidates multiple public and third-party data sources to help renters identify the most suitable borough on the Island of Montreal based on their individual priorities.

  • Simplify rental location decision-making
  • Compare Montreal boroughs using standardized and objective indicators
  • Prioritize factors based on individual needs (e.g., affordability vs. commute time)
  • Identify boroughs that offer the best overall balance of cost, accessibility, safety, and quality of life
  • Support landlords to determine competitive rental pricing based on their apartment’s location.

The dashboard should recommend at least three locations for users, ranked from top 1 to 3, and provide explanations for why each location was selected.

Proposed Solution

The project aims to gather data for eight key criteria: price, safety, commute time, walkability, public transportation, school availability, secondary school grades, and daycare availability.

The methodology is based on ranking Montreal locations for each criterion and scoring them according to both their rank and the relevance assigned by the user. Users can easily select the criteria most important to them and assign a weight— ranging from zero, low, medium, moderate, to high—to indicate its importance.

Once the criteria and weights are set, the dashboard highlights the top three recommended locations and provides explanations for why each location was selected. Users can explore different configurations to identify the location that best fits their needs. Additionally, the dashboard reveals less obvious characteristics of boroughs that may be important for decision-making.

Risk Analysis

Developing and implementing this project involves several potential risks, including:

  • Data Quality: Incomplete or inconsistent data from multiple sources could affect accuracy. Mitigation: validate, clean, and standardize data.
  • Technical/Platform: Microsoft Fabric trial limitations may restrict storage or performance. Mitigation: prioritize core features and optimize models.
  • Time & Resources: Single developer handling all roles may impact timelines. Mitigation: focus on core functionality and set realistic milestones.
  • User Experience: Weighting and ranking system may be confusing. Mitigation: provide clear instructions, tooltips, and examples.
  • Analytical Bias: Certain criteria may dominate rankings. Mitigation: ensure transparency and test multiple weighting scenarios.

Implementation Plan

The project is divided into three phases.

Phase 1 – Requirements gathering and data collection

  • Identify the requirements for the projects
  • Identify data sources
  • Define KPIs and methodologies to rank locations
  • Define project scope, limitations, and deadlines
  • Planning the architecture and dashboard visualization target

Phase 2 – Development

  • Gathering data
  • Develop medallion architecture and presentations layer
  • Develop PBI semantic model
  • Create KPIs, metrics, and dashboard
  • Stablish refresh schedule and method
  • Create development pipelines
  • Build project documentation

Phase 3 – Integration and testing

  • Validate KPIs and dashboard insights
  • Integrate dashboard to GitHub and make it available to public access
  • Integrate devop pipeline to GitHub

Project Continuous Development

There are many additional insights that can be incorporated into this project; therefore, continuous review of the project’s objectives, requirements, and KPIs is essential. The next phase of dashboard development will focus on expanding the criteria used to evaluate rental locations.

The following dimensions are planned for inclusion in the next version:

  • Household income by location
  • Age group distribution
  • Official language by location
  • Availability of hospitals and healthcare centers

In a subsequent version, machine learning techniques will be applied to identify relationships between these criteria and rental prices. While the current dashboard supports renters in identifying locations based on their personal priorities, the next iteration will also support property owners by estimating expected rental prices based on location-specific characteristics.

From the development perspective, the project will adopt Power BI Developer Mode with Git integration to support collaborative development and establish a scalable, multi-developer analytics framework.

Conclusion

Finding a suitable borough in Montreal is challenging for renters due to fragmented information and limited visibility into location-related issues. This solution addresses the problem by providing a unified, data-driven dashboard that helps tenants evaluate and compare locations based on key criteria such as price, safety, accessibility, transportation, commute time, and education services.

By prioritizing their needs, users receive clear recommendations for the top three locations that best match their preferences, along with explanations and access to rental listings. The platform reduces decision risk and enables more confident, informed rental choice.

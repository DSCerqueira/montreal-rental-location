Case Study

Data-driven rental location decision support for Montreal

1. Case Study Definition

This case study focuses on helping individuals, families, and shared households identify the borough on the Island of Montreal that best matches their rental needs. By combining housing prices, safety, accessibility, commute time, and access to public services, the project delivers a data-driven decision-support dashboard to guide renters toward the most suitable location.

2. Problem Statement

Finding a suitable place to rent on the Island of Montreal is a complex decision for individuals, families, and shared households. Rental prices, safety, commute time, and access to essential services such as schools and daycare vary significantly across boroughs.

This information is often fragmented across multiple sources, making it difficult for renters to objectively compare locations and identify the area that best meets their personal needs and constraints.

3. Business Objective

The objective of this case study is to design and deliver a data-driven dashboard that consolidates multiple public and third-party data sources to help renters identify the most suitable borough on the Island of Montreal based on their individual priorities.

  • Simplify rental location decision-making
  • Compare Montreal boroughs using standardized and objective indicators
  • Prioritize factors based on individual needs (e.g., affordability versus commute time)
  • Identify boroughs that offer the best overall balance of cost, accessibility, safety, and quality of life
  • Support landlords in determining competitive rental pricing based on their apartment’s location

4. Target Users

  • Individuals looking for their first rental in Montreal
  • Families with children seeking access to schools and daycare
  • Shared households prioritizing affordability and commute time
  • Newcomers and people relocating within the city
  • Older adults seeking locations that best match their needs
  • Landlords seeking to determine competitive rental pricing based on location
  • Real estate agencies aiming to advise clients and set competitive rental pricing

5. Key Use Cases

  • Compare average rental prices by borough
  • Identify areas with better walkability and public transit access
  • Assess safety using infraction rates
  • Evaluate access to schools, hospitals, and daycare services
  • Rank boroughs based on user-defined weighting of indicators
  • Track rental price trends across boroughs
  • Identify neighborhoods that are affordable for tenants or more profitable for landlords

6. Dashboard Success Metrics (KPIs)

User Experience Metrics

  • Ability to identify a preferred borough in fewer steps
  • Clear understanding of why a borough scores higher or lower
  • Ease of filtering and comparing boroughs

Data Quality Metrics

  • Coverage of all Montreal boroughs
  • Consistency and transparency of data sources
  • Timely refresh of rental and public datasets

Analytical Value Metrics

  • Meaningful differentiation between boroughs
  • Logical ranking based on weighted indicators
  • Alignment between user priorities and output rankings
